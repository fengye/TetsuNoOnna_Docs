Thank you for buying **Tetsu No Onna👩**, the DC power board for NEC PC-FX. This guide will help you install it on your PC-FX.

# Prerequisites

You will need:

* A **12V power adapter** which is:

  * Centre positive
  * Has a barrel connector with **inner diameter = 2.5mm** and **outer diameter = 5.5mm**
  ![Ref_Adapter_Connector](images_docs/20260308_172853.jpg)
  * Rated **2.5A or above**
  ![Ref_Adapter_Spec](images_docs/20260308_172826.jpg)
* A **Torx T10 screwdriver** for Type A PC-FX (see [Different PC-FX Models](#different-pc-fx-models))
* A **standard Phillips screwdriver**
* Optionally, a **pair of tweezers** for handling small parts

# Test Your 12V Power Adapter with the Board

Before starting the installation, it is a good idea to verify that **Tetsu No Onna** works with your power adapter.

1. Plug the **large JST connector** into the board.
![Test_Plug_In](images_docs/20260308_174747.jpg)
2. Connect the **barrel connector** from the power adapter into the jack.
![Test_Power_Up](images_docs/20260308_174804.jpg)

You should see a **green LED illuminate immediately**.

If the LED does not light up:

* Check that the **JST connector is firmly seated**.
* Check the power connector fits nicely with the power socket. If the power connector has an extra big inner diameter(although not likely) the positive tip won't make contact with the power socket, so it won't provide power.
* Confirm that the power adapter provides **12V** and is rated for **at least 2.5A**.

# Installation

1. Turn the PC-FX **off** and **unplug the power adapter** from both the wall and the console before starting the installation.

2. Unscrew the four Torx screws using a **T10 screwdriver**.
   On **Type B models** these are ordinary Phillips screws.
![Step2](images_docs/20260308_140552.jpg)

3. Pull the **left side plate downward**, then **toward the back**, and finally **lift it up**.
![Step3](images_docs/20260308_140649.jpg)
![Step3_2](images_docs/20260308_140700.jpg)
![Step3_3](images_docs/20260308_140732.jpg)

4. Do the same for the **right side plate**.
![Step4](images_docs/20260308_140751.jpg)

5. Use your finger to **unclip the 3 tabs securing the left side of the front panel**.
![Step5](images_docs/20260308_140821.jpg)

6. Pull the panel apart slightly using **gentle force**. Be careful not to break the tabs on the opposite side.
![Step6](images_docs/20260308_140839.jpg)

7. Unclip the **other 3 tabs on the right side** securing the front panel.
![Step7](images_docs/20260308_140852.jpg)

8. The **front panel should now come off easily**.
![Step8](images_docs/20260308_140907.jpg)

9. Move to the **top side of the motherboard**. Use tweezers to remove the **CD-ROM drive data connector**.
   Pull the **connector tab**, not the flat wires. There is a plastic fastener that must be pulled **upwards (towards the top panel)** to release it.
![Step9](images_docs/20260308_141027.jpg)

10. Do the same for the **CD-ROM motor connector**.
![Step10](images_docs/20260308_141111.jpg)

11. Remove the **CD-ROM lid sensor connector**.
![Step11](images_docs/20260308_141131.jpg)

12. Use your finger to **unclip the 4 tabs securing the left side of the top panel**.
![Step12](images_docs/20260308_141151.jpg)

13. Do the same on the **right side**, then lift the **top panel gently**.
    If all three CD-ROM connectors are disconnected, nothing should block the removal.
![Step13](images_docs/20260308_141215.jpg)
![Step13_2](images_docs/20260308_141252.jpg)

14. Turn to the **back of the console** and use a Phillips screwdriver to remove the **two screws securing the back panel and video connectors**.
![Step14](images_docs/20260308_141341.jpg)
![Step14_2](images_docs/20260308_141419.jpg)

15. Use your finger to **unclip the 3 tabs on the right side of the back panel**.
    Do the same on the **left side**. The back panel should now come off easily.
![Step15](images_docs/20260308_141511.jpg)

16. Gently **pull the power cord out of its holder** before removing the PSU.
![Step16](images_docs/20260308_142021.jpg)

17. Now turn to the **motherboard and PSU area**.
![Step17](images_docs/20260308_142337.jpg)

18. Remove the **8 Phillips screws securing the PSU and the motherboard metal shield**.
![Step18](images_docs/20260308_142357.jpg)

19. Carefully **lift the motherboard metal shield**.
![Step19](images_docs/20260308_142505.jpg)

20. It is recommended to use tweezers to **gently move the wires away from the capacitors**.
![Step20](images_docs/20260308_142531.jpg)

21. Unplug the **4-pin connector** from the PSU.
![Step21](images_docs/20260308_142555.jpg)

22. Unplug the **5-pin connector** from the PSU.
![Step22](images_docs/20260308_142604.jpg)

23. Carefully **remove the PSU**. Note that the **power cord is attached to it**.
![Step23](images_docs/20260308_142623.jpg)

24. Examine the PSU.
    If you own a **Type B PC-FX**, the PSU will look different and **will not have metal shielding**.
    If that is the case, **skip to step 29**.
![Step24](images_docs/20260308_150000.jpg)

25. Remove the **Phillips screws holding the metal shields together**, then remove the original power board.
    If the console was recently powered on, wait a **few minutes** before touching the board to avoid a shock.
![Step25](images_docs/20260308_142658.jpg)
![Step25_2](images_docs/20260308_142724.jpg)
![Step25_3](images_docs/20260308_142835.jpg)
![Step25_4](images_docs/20260308_142857.jpg)

26. Place **Tetsu No Onna** into the metal shield.
![Step26](images_docs/20260308_142938.jpg)

27. Ensure the **orientation of the shield opening is correct** so the two connectors are visible from the outside.
![Step27](images_docs/20260308_142957.jpg)

28. Reinstall and **tighten the two screws**.
![Step28](images_docs/20260308_143022.jpg)
![Step28_1](images_docs/20260308_143053.jpg)

29. Connect the **large JST connector** to the back of the board (where the AC cord previously connected).
    The **PSU assembly is now complete**.
![Step29](images_docs/20260308_143236.jpg)
![Step29_1](images_docs/20260308_143250.jpg)

30. Place the reassembled **Tetsu No Onna PSU** back into position.
    Make sure the **5-pin connector cable is routed beside the PSU and not trapped underneath it**.
![Step30](images_docs/20260308_143118.jpg)

31. Reconnect the **4-pin** and **5-pin** connectors.
![Step31](images_docs/20260308_143140.jpg)

32. Install the **two screws on the left** to secure the PSU.
![Step32](images_docs/20260308_143402.jpg)

33. Reinstall the **motherboard metal shield** and fasten the **remaining 6 screws**.
![Step33](images_docs/20260308_143427.jpg)

34. Pay extra attention to the **bottom-right screw**.
    You may need tweezers to properly secure the **grounding wire**.
![Step34](images_docs/20260308_143454.jpg)

35. Insert the **3D-printed power socket shell** into the back plate.
![Step35](images_docs/20260308_143619.jpg)

36. Push the **back plate with the power socket** into the main body. It should **fit snugly**.
![Step36](images_docs/20260308_143643.jpg)
![Step36_2](images_docs/20260308_143710.jpg)

37. Connect the **power adapter** to the rear socket.
    The **green LED should illuminate**, indicating that power is available.
![Step37](images_docs/20260308_143901.jpg)

38. Press the **blue switch on the front panel**.
    The **red and blue LEDs should illuminate**, indicating that **Tetsu No Onna is now supplying power to the console**.
![Step38](images_docs/20260308_143954.jpg)
![Step38_2](images_docs/20260308_144041.jpg)
![Step38_3](images_docs/20260308_144106.jpg)

39. Turn the console **off again**, then reassemble the console by following the steps **in reverse starting from step 15**.

40. **Tada! Your installation is complete.**

# Different PC-FX Models

### Type A

* A **power specification sticker** is located on the back.
* The **BIOS chip on the motherboard has an “A” sticker**.
* The **original PSU has a metal shield**.

### Type B

* Power specifications are **moulded directly into the shell**.
* The **BIOS chip on the motherboard has a “B” sticker**.
* The **original PSU does not have a metal shield**.
